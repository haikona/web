(2014-01-31) In question 4 of the midterm I used \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) in the statement of the differential equation, but talk about \(t\) as the independent variable in part b). This is my error: everything should be in terms of \(t\), so the differential equation should be $$\frac{dy}{dt} = \sin^2(y)-K$$ My apologies if this confused anyone during the midterm. I'm raising the regrade point cap for question 4 to 10 points, so you can now get full grade for this question if your writeup is fully correct.

(2014-01-31) MIDTERM 1 MAKEUP GUIDELINES:
I'm allowing you to redo questions 4 and 5 of Midterm 1 as a take-home assignment, to be handed in Monday 3 Feb at the beginning of class. I will grade both questions 4 and 5 out of a maximum of 10 points total. These scores will then replace your existing scores for those two questions (only if your new scores are higher; I won't ever adjust your score downwards).

For each question to be eligible to be graded you must write up full solutions to all parts of that question, not just the parts where you lost points. This includes the third part of question 5; I will not regrade your question 5 if you have not attempted part c). If you feel you did well enough on one of the questions you may of course just submit a solution to the other one, and you are welcome to not submit any rewrites if you are happy with your scores on both questions.

I encourage everyone who got less than full grade on questions 4 and/or 5 to submit new solutions, as both problems are exercises in inferring the behavior of a dynamic system through analyzing its governing differential equation; this is one of the most crucial skills you should learn in this course. I encourage you to collaborate and figure out the solutions in groups together; however, as always please acknowledge anyone you worked with in writing at the top of you writeup. Furthermore, even if you collaborate with others to figure out the problem solutions you must write up your solutions in your own words; I won't accept the exact same solution copied into multiple write-ups.

Finally, because this is open book with much less time pressure, I'll be grading your submissions more strictly than I did for questions 4 and 5 on the midterm. I urge you therefore to double-check your solutions for correctness.
