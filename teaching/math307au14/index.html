(2014-11-14) On Wednesday I gave two equivalent formulae on the board for $\omega_{\text{max}}$, the resonant frequency (the frequency at which the forced response amplitude is maximized) in a forced oscillatory system with damping. In one of them I included an error. I had stated that $\omega_{\text{max}} = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m} - \frac{\gamma^2}{2km}}$; however, the second term under the square root sign should have a $m^2$ in the denominator instead of $km$. The correct formula for the resonant frequency is $$ \omega_{\text{max}} = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m} - \frac{\gamma^2}{2m^2}} = \omega_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{\gamma^2}{2km}}$$ where $m$ is the mass of the object being modeled, $\gamma$ the damping constant, $k$ the spring constant, and $\omega_0 = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$ the natural frequency of the system.
