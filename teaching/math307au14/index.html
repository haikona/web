(2014-10-09) A reminder that Midterm 1 takes place next Wednesday October 15 during class. The work covered in the midterm is all the work covered in lectures and homeworks up to and including Friday October 10. More specifically, you are expected to know the following:

  • How to solve separable first-order DEs (Boyce 2.2)
  • How to solve linear first-order DEs (Boyce 2.1)
  • How to solve and solve a first-order word problem (Boyce 2.3)
  • How to determine Existence and Uniqueness (Boyce 2.4)
  • How to interpret a slope field (Boyce 1.1)
  • How to analyze autonomous equations qualitatively (Boyce 2.5)
  • How to use Euler's Method (Boyce 2.7)
You are allowed one two-sided handwritten notesheet to bring in to the midterm, and use of a scientific calculator. Typed notes and graphing calculators are not permitted.

(2014-09-30) You are expected to show your working on any question in the homework that’s more than a little involved (the same goes for midterms and the final when you get there). The exact definition of “more than a little involved” may vary from person to person, but the rule of thumb is this: if you can’t arrive at the correct answer in your head in a few seconds, you should be writing down your working as part of the solution writeup. The course grader and I reserve the right to dock points from your homeworks/exams if you fail to show your working in a given problem.

(2014-09-26) Homework submission format clarification:

  • Homeworks are to be written or typed up and handed to me in class on Wednesdays. Please make sure your submissions are neat and legible, and have your name and student number at the top of each page.
  • Please staple any pages you submit together. If you submit more than one page for your homework and don't staple the pages together you will lose 2 points for that homework (out of 20 - i.e. 10%). I will try to bring a stapler to class on Wednesdays, so if you forget beforehand you can staple your homeworks then.
